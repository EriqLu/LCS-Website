Anthony Avina - Class of 2012 He joined LCSC along with Fernanda in Spring '07. He joined mostly to get to know more people in CS and to get involved. He is proud to be able to see more Latino/a students enroll in Computer Science. Our major takes a lot of work, as do many others, and he feel that if any students are in trouble with homework or anything they can talk to a member from our club to help them through. He is confident that one day there won't be any more underrepresented ethnicities present in our or any other major. More information about her position and the industry he works at soon.

Alba J. Rico - Class of 2011 Her concentration is in Databases and she was the Secretary for the Spring 2011. She joined the Latino Computer Science Club to motivate more Latino and minority students to join Computer Science and increase the number of Latinos in the department. She really enjoyed her experience as Secretary and she wish she could have applied for a officer position long time ago so she could have contributed much more. Even though that she graduated, she would like to keep in touch with the club and keep contributing in whatever she can. So do not hesitate in contact her at (joycerico85@gmail.com) in case you need an advise or for any reason. She will be happy to help. More information about her postion and industry she works at soon.

Fernanda Mendes - Class of 2010 My specialization is in Human-Computer Interaction. I was the President of LCSC for Fall'08 to Spring '09. I have been in the Latino/a Computer Science club since it was founded in the Spring of 2007. I joined the club because I knew that my involvement would have an impact in the direction of the club's future. As our Computer Science department strives to become more diverse, I enjoy being a part of a community whose main goal is to support and retain underrepresented students in CS. What I like the most about LCSC is the opportunity to meet other students with similar backgrounds and interests as me
